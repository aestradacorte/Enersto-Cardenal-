Ernesto Cardenal

Poet • Priest • Prophet

Biographical Details

Born in Granada, Nicaragua (1925), Ernesto Cardenal studied literature and theology across Latin America and the U.S. He later joined the Trappist Monastery in Kentucky and was ordained a Catholic priest in 1965. He founded the Solentiname community focused on art, prayer, and justice.

Power & Oppression

Cardenal joined the Sandinista movement to resist the Somoza dictatorship, becoming Nicaragua’s Minister of Culture. His political involvement led to his suspension from priesthood by Pope John Paul II, a ban that was lifted in 2019 by Pope Francis.

Theological Insights

A key voice in Liberation Theology, Cardenal emphasized God’s preferential option for the poor. His work The Gospel in Solentiname captures radical gospel reflections calling for justice, love, and solidarity with the oppressed.

Legacy

Nominated for the Nobel Prize in Literature and known globally for his poetic and political contributions, Cardenal remains a symbol of resistance and prophetic faith. His writings continue to inspire justice-minded Christians today.
